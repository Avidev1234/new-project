What Is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis is a condition brought on by elevated blood glucose levels, sometimes referred to as glucose levels. The pancreas secretes the hormone insulin, which facilitates the uptake of glucose by your cells for use as fuel. When you have Tuberculosis, your body either produces insufficient amounts of insulin or doesn't use it correctly. After that, glucose remains in your circulation and does not enter your cells.

The ayurvedic management of Tuberculosis aids in the treatment of disorder from the core. Tuberculosis is also associated with increased risk of heart, kidney, and eye diseases.

Symptoms of Tuberculosis

Type 1 Tuberculosis

The body mistakenly attacks itself is thought to be the aetiology of type 1 Tuberculosis. Your body's production of insulin is stopped by this reaction. Type 1 Tuberculosis symptoms frequently appear suddenly. Typically, it is discovered in kids, teenagers, and young adults.

Autoimmune Juvenile Onset

Type 2 Tuberculosis

The human body struggles to properly utilise insulin in type 2 Tuberculosis, making it difficult to maintain normal blood sugar levels. The majority of diabetics (90-95%) are type 2. It takes years to develop, and adults are typically diagnosed with it.

Most Common Adult Onset

Gestational Tuberculosis

Women who have never had Tuberculosis before get the condition during pregnancy. If you have gestational Tuberculosis, your unborn child may be more susceptible to health issues. After your baby is born, gestational Tuberculosis typically disappears.

Pregnancy Related Temporary
